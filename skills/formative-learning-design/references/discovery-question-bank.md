# Discovery Question Bank

Use these only as needed. Do not dump them all on the user.

## Outcomes

- What do you want learners to be able to notice, decide, or do differently afterward?
- Is the target knowledge, judgment, collaboration, identity formation, or something else?
- What would count as strong performance in this exercise?

## Learners

- Who are the learners, really: novice students, practitioners, faculty, leaders?
- What do they already know?
- What misconceptions or habits are likely to show up?
- What might feel risky, confusing, or alienating to them?

## Authenticity

- What real-world situation is this simulating?
- What makes that situation genuinely hard in practice?
- What ambiguity, politics, or tradeoff should be preserved?
- What would make the exercise feel fake to insiders?

## Activity Shape

- Why a simulation, case, role play, or document review rather than a discussion or mini-lecture?
- What must participants produce, recommend, or defend by the end?
- What information should be shared, withheld, or revealed later?
- What should each team know that the others do not?

## Facilitation

- How much time is available for setup, activity, and debrief?
- Will there be one facilitator or several?
- How much facilitation complexity is realistic?
- What should facilitators listen for during the exercise?

## Debrief

- What reflection or debrief should happen immediately after?
- What do you want learners to compare across teams?
- How will participants connect the exercise back to practice?

## Inclusion and Access

- How will learners with different backgrounds, confidence levels, or communication styles participate well?
- Are there multiple ways to engage with the materials and show understanding?
- What prior knowledge needs scaffolding instead of being assumed?

## AI Boundaries

- Where should AI extend capacity rather than replace it?
- What parts of the work must remain human-authored?
- How will learners surface their process rather than only their final answer?

## Logistics

- Does this need to live in Canvas, Google Docs, slides, print handouts, or all three?
- Do you want clean handout copy, editable draft copy, or facilitator notes with annotations?
- Is this a one-off experience, a reusable template, or the start of a larger library?
